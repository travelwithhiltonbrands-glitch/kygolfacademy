About

Golf instruction with clarity, logic, and purpose.

I grew up in Blacksburg, Virginia, a small college town in the Blue Ridge Mountains.

At age 17, I was introduced to golf through one of my best friends and met an incredible PGA Professional named Steve Prater, who remains one of the most influential mentors in my life. His encouragement and guidance went far beyond golf, and from that moment on, I was hooked.

Within a year, I became a single-digit handicap player and earned multiple top-10 finishes in tournaments across the nation. In high school, I joined the varsity golf team alongside several outstanding players, all coached by Steve. Together, we went on to win three consecutive Virginia state championships.

My path ultimately led me toward education, and I pursued business and engineering degrees from George Washington University and Cornell University. Despite that, my passion for golf never faded. In 2010, I earned my teaching license from the United States Golf Teachers Federation and have remained in good standing ever since.

I am currently a teaching professional at Pinecrest Golf Course in Alexandria, Virginia. I combine my playing experience with my engineering and analytical background to help students understand not just what they do in their golf swing, but why they do it.

I approach coaching in a systematic, logical way that helps golfers learn more efficiently and confidently. I look forward to the opportunity to share my knowledge and passion for the game with you.

Lessons available in both English and Korean.

Coaching Philosophy

Learn what your swing is doing, and why.

Good instruction should make golf feel simpler, not more confusing. My coaching focuses on helping students understand ball flight, body motion, sequencing, and practice habits in a way that builds confidence over time.

Whether you are just starting out or trying to sharpen performance, the goal is the same: clear communication, measurable progress, and a swing you can trust.
